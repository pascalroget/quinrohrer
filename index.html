What Happened

Quin Rohrer operated as a solar equipment broker through multiple LLC entities, primarily Globus Enterprises, LLC and Renewable Enterprises, both registered in Utah. His pattern was consistent: accept large wire payments for solar panels, provide excuses for delays, then disappear with the money.

In June 2023, Kinect Solar, LLC filed a federal lawsuit after Rohrer collected $252,913.50 for 1,519 solar panels that were never delivered. Rohrer didn't even respond to the lawsuit. The court entered a default judgment finding him liable for fraud, conversion, theft, and breach of contract.

Kinect Solar is not the only victim. Reports from the solar industry indicate multiple companies and brokers have been defrauded in the same manner, with total documented losses approaching half a million dollars.

Court Findings

The court found sufficient evidence to establish all of the following claims:

  • Fraud — Rohrer knowingly misrepresented his ability and intent to deliver solar equipment
  • Conversion — Funds wired for equipment were taken for personal use
  • Theft (Texas Theft Liability Act) — Unlawful appropriation of property
  • Fraudulent Inducement — False promises made to secure wire transfers
  • Breach of Contract — Equipment paid for was never delivered
  • Money Had and Received — Unjust retention of funds

The Pattern

Based on court documents and victim reports, Quin Rohrer's operation follows a consistent pattern:

  1. Contact — Approaches solar companies as an equipment broker, often through industry connections or online groups.
  2. Offer — Quotes competitive prices on solar panels or modules, typically for large orders ($50K–$250K+).
  3. Payment — Requests wire transfer to a business bank account (Globus Enterprises LLC or similar entity).
  4. Delay — After payment clears, provides a string of excuses: shipping delays, bank account issues, supply chain problems.
  5. Ghost — Eventually stops responding to calls, texts, and emails. Blocks phone numbers.
  6. Ignore — Does not respond to demand letters from attorneys or federal lawsuits.

In some cases, Rohrer has operated through intermediary brokers, creating a chain where the middleman also becomes a victim when Rohrer disappears with the funds.
